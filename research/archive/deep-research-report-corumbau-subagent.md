# Deep Research Report: BWB Corumbau Collaboration Opportunities

Last verified: 2026-06-25

## Summary

Corumbau/Prado-Bahia has the strongest documented collaboration patterns around traditional fishing livelihoods, coastal conservation, marine education, and regional technical education rather than a dense landscape of formal mutual-aid or makerspace organizations. The best-supported leads are connected to the Corumbau Marine Extractive Reserve: the Associação dos Pescadores de Cumuruxatiba - APEC, the RESEX deliberative council/ICMBio management structure, and nearby marine-conservation nonprofit Instituto Baleia Jubarte in the Costa das Baleias/Abrolhos region. A regional technical-education partner, IF Baiano Campus Teixeira de Freitas, may be the closest practical infrastructure lead for repair/build/training ideas, but its fit requires direct verification because no local makerspace or tool library was found in Corumbau or Prado.

    - Pair BWB volunteer capacity with APEC-identified needs for cleanup days, community meals, or mutual support around fishing-season disruptions.
  source_urls:
    - https://uc.socioambiental.org/pt-br/arp/2585
  research_notes: The source confirms APEC's formal relationship to ICMBio/RESEX land-use arrangements, but does not provide a current public website, current officers, or recent activity record. Verify whether APEC remains active, who is authorized to receive partnership inquiries, and whether Corumbau-based fishers prefer APEC, the RESEX council, or another local association as the first contact.
