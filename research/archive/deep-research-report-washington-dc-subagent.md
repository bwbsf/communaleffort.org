## Summary

Washington D.C. has strong collaboration potential in the two requested categories. For labor and worker-center work, the strongest patterns are worker-rights education, skilled-trades safety referrals, apprenticeship and workforce-development navigation, immigrant-worker outreach, and solidarity logistics that do not substitute unpaid volunteers for paid skilled labor. For resident and neighborhood-leader work, the strongest patterns are listening sessions with Advisory Neighborhood Commissions, resident-led project review, mutual-aid support, Ward-based outreach, and community-designed public-space, housing, youth, or resilience projects. Several leads have active 2026 web presence, but human review should verify current chapter relationships, partner capacity, safety policies, and whether proposed collaboration should be framed as volunteer support, co-hosting, resource distribution, or simply listening before outreach.
