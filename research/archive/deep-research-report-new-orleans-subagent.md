# Deep Research Report: BWB New Orleans Collaboration Opportunities

Last verified: 2026-06-24

## Summary

New Orleans has unusually strong BWB-aligned collaboration patterns around disaster preparedness, neighborhood-led recovery, mutual aid, creative public culture, climate adaptation, materials reuse, and practical volunteer logistics. The strongest leads are organizations with public collaboration pathways and local infrastructure: NOLA Ready for preparedness training and VOAD/volunteer alignment, Imagine Water Works for mutual-aid and storm-resource coordination, SOUL for urban forestry and neighborhood resilience, Second Harvest for food logistics and disaster food response, the Green Project and Glass Half Full for reuse and waste-diversion infrastructure, lowernine.org for resident-centered rebuilding, New Orleans Airlift for participatory arts, CBNO for neighborhood civic leadership, and New Orleans Public Library for tool-library, REACH Center, and public-programming capacity. These are research leads only; integration should verify current activity, collaboration boundaries, insurance/permit needs, group volunteer capacity, and whether each organization wants BWB contact before outreach.
