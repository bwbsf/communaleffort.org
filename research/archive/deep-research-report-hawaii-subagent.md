# Deep Research Report: BWB Hawaii Collaboration Opportunities

Last verified: 2026-06-24

## Summary

The strongest collaboration patterns for BWB Hawaii are disaster-readiness coordination, practical food and supply distribution, environmental education and waste-diversion projects, neighborhood-scale listening, and public education through libraries and community convening spaces. Hawaii has several active statewide or island-specific organizations with clear public programs, current websites, and plausible collaboration pathways: Hawaiʻi Foodbank and Aloha United Way for direct service/referrals; Maui Rapid Response and Mālama Map for emergent mutual-aid coordination; Kōkua Hawaiʻi Foundation and Re-use Hawaiʻi for environmental stewardship, reuse, and circular-economy projects; Hawaiʻi Contemporary for participatory arts and place-based cultural programming; Hawaiʻi Community Foundation for grant/funder context; HI-EMA and Honolulu DEM for formal preparedness alignment; HCATF for skilled-build/safety-training pathways; and the Hawaiʻi State Public Library System plus Oʻahu Neighborhood Boards for trusted public gathering and civic engagement.

Validation limits: this report used public web sources available on 2026-06-24 and did not contact organizations, verify private partnership policies, confirm staff capacity, inspect current insurance/permit requirements, or localize source evidence into `evidence/`. Before importing any opportunity into chapter front matter, maintainers should run the integration playbook, verify the lead is still active, confirm geographic fit for the chapter's intended island/community, and re-check each organization's current collaboration/contact preferences.
