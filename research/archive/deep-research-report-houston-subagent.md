## Summary

Houston's strongest collaboration patterns are disaster recovery, neighborhood-scale mutual aid, food access, climate and water resilience, public arts/community activation, worker safety, creative reuse, and civic engagement through city-recognized neighborhood channels. The strongest evidence-backed leads are public-facing organizations with active Houston infrastructure: Project Row Houses for community-engaged arts and direct-action placemaking, Houston Food Bank for food distribution and volunteer logistics, Bayou City Waterkeeper for water justice and flood resilience, Greater Houston Community Foundation for philanthropic infrastructure, Fe y Justicia Worker Center for day-laborer and domestic-worker safety work, Magpies & Peacocks for textile reuse and creative workforce programming, the City of Houston Department of Neighborhoods for resident-facing public programs, TXRX Labs for maker infrastructure, West Street Recovery for mutual aid and disaster recovery, BakerRipley for community services and centers, the City of Houston Super Neighborhoods program for resident leadership pathways, and Houston Public Library for distributed learning and gathering infrastructure.

Research limits: this pass relied on public web sources reachable during the session, with preference for official organization pages.
