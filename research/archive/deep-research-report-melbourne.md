## 1. Short summary of strongest collaboration patterns for Melbourne

Melbourne’s strongest BWB collaboration patterns are practical and place-based: food relief and hygiene support with large volunteer-ready service providers; repair, tool-library, circular-economy, and maker partnerships for build days and reuse events; arts-led public participation through Footscray, Collingwood, North Melbourne, and multicultural arts networks; neighbourhood-house listening sessions for grounded local priorities; and public-agency coordination around grants, preparedness, and community resilience. The best near-term opportunities appear to be: a food/supply sorting volunteer day with Foodbank Victoria or FareShare; a repair/reuse event with Brunswick Tool Library, Green Collect, or City of Melbourne Libraries; a community arts/resilience workshop with Footscray Community Arts, Arts House, or Collingwood Yards; and a neighbourhood listening-and-build cycle hosted through neighbourhood houses.
