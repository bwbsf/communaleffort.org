# Deep Research Report: BWB New Mexico Collaboration Opportunities

Last verified: 2026-06-24

## Summary

BWB New Mexico appears to be a statewide working group with a documented civic-project orientation and at least one historic homeless-youth survival-support project in Santa Fe. The strongest collaboration patterns are therefore practical, mobile, and networked rather than tied to one neighborhood: food access and shelter partners in Albuquerque and statewide networks; Santa Fe-based community arts/storytelling and compost/local-food infrastructure; Albuquerque makerspaces and workforce-training institutions for build days; and public-sector emergency/neighborhood coordination for preparedness or resilience events. The best-supported leads below emphasize organizations with public programs, physical facilities, volunteer pathways, or statewide service networks that could plausibly support BWB-style volunteer mobilization.

Validation limits: this pass used public web evidence available on 2026-06-24 and did not contact organizations, verify insurance/permit requirements, confirm current staff capacity, or validate whether BWB New Mexico is currently active beyond its public BWB page. Some official sites were inaccessible or sparse through the research environment, so lower-confidence leads are marked in `research_notes` or omitted.
