## Summary

Galveston's strongest collaboration patterns are coastal stewardship, food access, public arts activation, neighborhood resilience, and accessible civic gathering spaces. The strongest evidence-backed leads are public-facing organizations with active local infrastructure: Galveston Arts Center for ArtWalk and arts education, Galveston County Food Bank for food distribution and volunteer logistics, Artist Boat for habitat restoration and coastal education, Groundswell for food access and community gardens, the City of Galveston and Galveston Park Board for public-space and resilience coordination, Galveston Urban Ministries for neighborhood relationship-based poverty work, Galveston College for learning/workforce facilities, and the Moody Foundation for Texas grantmaking rooted in Galveston.

Research limits: this pass relied on public web sources reachable during the session. I found weak public evidence for a Galveston-specific mutual-aid group and for a labor union or worker center with clear public civic-collaboration pathways, so those categories are listed under `no_good_leads_found`. Some promising leads, especially Galveston Housing Plus, Rosenberg Library, Family Service Center, and local neighborhood associations, may deserve follow-up but were not included as YAML-ready opportunities because official pages were inaccessible or source support was too thin during this pass.
