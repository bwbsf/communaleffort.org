# Deep Research Report: BWB Utah Collaboration Opportunities

Last verified: 2026-06-25

## Summary

The strongest Utah collaboration patterns are practical volunteer mobilization, public-facing preparedness education, creative reuse, food and basic-needs logistics, and neighborhood-scale civic convening. The most actionable leads are statewide or Salt Lake Valley-based organizations with clear volunteer, donation, training, public-programming, or community-partner pathways: Utah Food Bank, The Road Home, VOA Utah, TreeUtah, Clever Octopus, Craft Lake City, Community Foundation of Utah, IBEW Local 354, Salt Lake City Emergency Management, Salt Lake City Community Councils, and Salt Lake Community College's Thayne Center. I did not find a durable, official, non-social-only source for a Utah mutual-aid collective that met the evidence standard during this pass, so that category is listed under `no_good_leads_found`.

## Validation limits

- This report is a research draft, not an integration decision; every opportunity should be rechecked against the integration playbook before importing into chapter front matter.
- I prioritized official organization and public-agency websites and avoided social-media-only leads except as future follow-up.
- I did not contact organizations, confirm partnership interest, verify insurance/permitting constraints, or validate current staff capacity.
- Some Utah-wide leads are centered in Salt Lake County/Salt Lake City because that is where public evidence and collaboration infrastructure were strongest.
- Mutual-aid research was limited by lack of durable official sources; direct local referrals may surface better leads than web search.
