# Collaboration Opportunities for BWB Fly Ranch

## Strongest collaboration patterns

Fly Ranch is a remote site in Washoe County, about 24 miles north of Gerlach, and Burners Without Borders describes its Fly Ranch gatherings as intentionally small because the land’s infrastructure and ecosystem can currently host only 99 people at a time. That geography matters: the best collaboration pattern is not a single-city strategy, but a **two-ring network** made up of hyperlocal Gerlach/Black Rock partners for land literacy, resident trust, and town-scale logistics, plus Reno/Sparks organizations for grants, tools, volunteer systems, and direct-service infrastructure. citeturn0search15turn22search4turn23search3

The chapter also already has a strong precedent for work that blends ecology, culture, and practical making. Its public chapter page points to prior projects with the Pyramid Lake Paiute Tribe and to a maker-container concept, while BWB and Fly Ranch describe the site as a place for work weekends, skill sharing, and project prototyping. That makes the best-fit leads the ones that can help with **rural stewardship, public interpretation, food and material logistics, small-scale arts activation, skilled training, and community listening** rather than generic nonprofit outreach. citeturn22search0turn22search6turn22search5

The most compelling local pattern is to use **Gerlach-based institutions first** when community trust or place knowledge is essential. Friends of Black Rock-High Rock already manages Fly Ranch nature walks with Burning Man and runs a Gerlach visitor center and artist/residency programs; the Gerlach/Empire Citizens Advisory Board and Gerlach General Improvement District are the clearest public-facing resident forums; and a handful of very local businesses and community facilities can support meals, supplies, or meeting space. Around that local ring, Reno/Sparks partners can add stronger operational backbone in food distribution, fiscal sponsorship, makerspace access, apprenticeship-style training, and inclusive service referrals. citeturn23search0turn23search1turn19search0turn3search0turn25search3turn32view0turn32view1

In practical terms, the strongest collaboration shapes for Fly Ranch are these: **stewardship and interpretation** with Black Rock-area groups; **small public workshops and placemaking** with arts and makerspace partners; **mutual-aid and direct-service support** routed through durable Reno/Sparks providers; **project sponsorship and small-grant pathways** through Northern Nevada philanthropic infrastructure; and **resident-informed pilots** that are floated publicly in Gerlach before they scale. citeturn19search9turn24view0turn2search3turn12search11turn15search0turn13search0
